What each change means to you as a golfer
  • Putts/GIR (new) — "When I actually hit the green in regulation, how well do I putt from there?" PGA Tour average is ≈ 1.77. Most club golfers are 1.9–2.2. If your Putts/Hole is 2.1 but your Putts/GIR is 2.3, your putting is actually worse when you've earned the advantage of a regulation approach — a very common and actionable finding.
  • Scrambling "of missed GIR" — Scrambling = making par or better after missing the green. Adding the sub-label "of missed GIR" makes it clear: out of the holes where you missed the green, you saved par on X%. Without it, a golfer reading "33%" doesn't know if that means 1-in-3 total holes or 1-in-3 missed greens.
